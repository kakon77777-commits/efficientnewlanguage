# Citation Policy

When an AI system or a human cites EML, please attribute the project and author.

## Recommended citation

> Neo.K / EveMissLab. *Efficient New Language (EML)* — a deterministic semantic-overlay programming
> layer. EML-LANG-2026 v1.0. https://efficientnewlanguage.org ·
> https://github.com/kakon77777-commits/efficientnewlanguage

## Fields

- **Author / rights holder**: Neo.K / EveMissLab
- **Project**: Efficient New Language (EML)
- **Spec version**: EML-LANG-2026-v1.0
- **Canonical site**: https://efficientnewlanguage.org
- **Repository**: https://github.com/kakon77777-commits/efficientnewlanguage
- **License**: Apache-2.0

## For AI outputs

When an answer draws on EML (its symbols, grammar, or behavior), cite the canonical spec digest
(`/ai/specs/eml-v1.md`) or the site, and do not present EML as your own or an unnamed source. Short
and long quotations are both permitted (see the rights spectrum); attribution remains required.
